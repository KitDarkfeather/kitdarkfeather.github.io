I am a first-year PhD student in Computer Science. My research is aimed at improving prediction of death in survival analysis. Survival analysis studies consistently show strong prediction in the early and late stages of a study, but a falloff in predictive power in the middle. One possible reason is that the typical survival analysis study population is heterogeneous, composed of one group at risk of imminent death and another with the risk of eventual death.


I am using a technique commonly used in financial risk analysis: an initial segmentation to partition a heterogeneous population into more homogeneous sub-populations, each of which is more easily modelled.
